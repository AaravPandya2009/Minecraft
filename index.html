Part 01

The Component Library

Every redstone machine, from a two-block door opener to a raid farm the size of a village, is built from the same handful of parts. Learn what each one actually does before combining them.

Power Source Logic Sensor Actuator Transport Output Input Utility

Redstone Dust

Transport

The wire. Carries a signal outward from a source, losing 1 strength per block, up to 15 blocks before it needs boosting.

Redstone Torch

Power Source · Logic

An always-on power source that also inverts signals — it switches off when the block behind it is powered. This single behavior is the basis of every NOT gate.

Repeater

Logic

Delays a pulse by 1–4 ticks, refreshes signal strength back to full, and can "lock" — freezing its output when side-powered by another repeater.

Comparator

Logic

Compares two input signals, or subtracts one from the other. Also reads a chest, hopper, or barrel's fill level as a raw signal strength — the basis of every sorting system.

Observer

Sensor

Watches the block directly in front of it and fires a single one-tick pulse out its back the instant that block changes — a crop growing, a piston moving, anything.

Piston

Actuator

Extends to push up to 12 blocks in a line. The core "mover" behind doors, hidden entrances, and every flying machine.

Sticky Piston

Actuator

Same as a piston, but its honeycomb face drags the pushed block back on retraction — enabling reversible movement and compact pulling mechanisms.

Dispenser

Actuator

Uses whatever item it holds — fires arrows, splashes water or lava, plants crops, ignites TNT. The workhorse of most automated farms and cannons.

Dropper

Actuator · Transport

Ejects a single item into whatever is in front of it, rather than using the item — the gentle cousin of the dispenser, used for precise item transfer.

Hopper

Transport

Funnels items downward or sideways into a container. Chained hoppers form the "conveyor belt" backbone of nearly every collection system, and can be locked by a redstone signal.

Redstone Lamp

Output

A light source that only glows while powered. Pure output — used constantly as a status indicator for "is this farm running" style dashboards.

Lever & Button

Input

A lever holds a permanent on/off state; a button fires a short pulse (stone: 1 tick, wood: 1.5s, cross: even longer). The manual override on almost every machine.

Pressure Plate

Sensor

Detects weight — a player, mob, item, or minecart — and outputs a signal. Wood plates trigger on anything; stone only on players and mobs.

Tripwire Hook

Sensor

A hook-and-string pair that detects any entity crossing the line — silent, hard to spot, and a favorite for hidden traps and item-sorting triggers.

Note Block

Output

Plays a pitched sound when powered. Beyond music, engineers use its distinct click as an audible debug signal for testing clocks and timings.

Daylight Sensor

Sensor

Outputs a signal proportional to sky light level — strong at noon, off at night. Can be right-clicked to invert, making it a simple built-in day/night switch.

Target Block

Sensor

Emits a signal strength based on how precisely a projectile hits its bullseye — the only block that turns archery accuracy into redstone data.

Redstone Block

Power Source

A constant, maximum-strength power source that also counts as a solid block — often pushed by pistons to deliver power exactly where and when it's needed.

Slime & Honey Block

Utility

Slime blocks stick to almost any adjacent block (except honey); honey blocks stick only to slime. Pistons using this pair can drag entire structures — the basis of every flying machine.

Part 02

Advanced Builds

Four community-designed machines that push the component library to its limits. Each is built entirely from vanilla blocks and vanilla mechanics — no command blocks, no glitches, no mods — and is credited to the player who designed it.

The Orbital Strike Cannon

Designercubicmetre
Best inJava Edition
DifficultyExtreme
CategoryTNT Artillery

How it works

This is a battery of many parallel TNT cannons wired to fire in a single synchronized volley at a fixed target. Each individual "barrel" is a classic water-housed cannon: a charge of TNT sits in water so its blast doesn't destroy the structure, and detonating it launches a second piece of "shot" TNT into the air, which is timed to explode mid-flight over the target instead of underfoot. Stacking dozens of these barrels side by side, fed automatically and fired together, turns a toy into an area-denial weapon capable of leveling a base in seconds — all using intended TNT physics, not duplication or block-placement glitches.

Build outline

  1. Construct a single water-housed barrel: a sealed chamber of "charge" TNT submerged in water, with a "shot" block of TNT positioned to be launched by that charge's explosion.
  2. Wire a dispenser or direct redstone contact to ignite the charge, and tune a repeater delay so the shot TNT detonates at the desired distance instead of at your feet.
  3. Duplicate the barrel dozens of times across a firing line, all aimed at the same target point.
  4. Run a single "fire control" bus of redstone dust, boosted with repeaters and redstone-block relays over the long distance, so every barrel receives the trigger signal on the same tick.
  5. Feed each barrel's dispenser automatically from a central TNT silo using hopper minecart lines, so the battery can be fired repeatedly without manual reloading.
  6. Add a comparator-based ammo check that disables the fire-control bus when the magazine runs low, preventing a dry, useless volley.

Role of each component

PartRole in this machine
Redstone DustCarries the fire-control signal down every barrel row.
RepeaterTunes each barrel's ignition-to-launch delay, which sets its firing range.
Redstone TorchHolds each barrel in an inverted "safe" state until the master signal arrives.
DispenserLoads or ignites the charge TNT in each barrel.
Hopper MinecartAutomatically resupplies every barrel's TNT from a central magazine.
ComparatorReads the magazine's fill level and locks firing when ammo runs out.
Redstone BlockRelays the fire signal at full strength across long distances.
LeverThe operator's master trigger for the entire battery.

Relies only on core TNT-in-water physics and redstone signal transmission, so it holds up across most modern Java releases; large builds like this are typically staged in creative or with schematics before being reproduced in survival.

Automatic Iron Golem Farm

Design lineagegnembon
Best inJava 1.16 – 1.21
DifficultyModerate
CategoryMob Farm

How it works

Villagers that see a zombie-type mob nearby, but can't reach it, become "afraid." If enough villagers in the same village have enough beds claimed and no golem currently exists, the game periodically spawns an iron golem to protect them. This farm cages a permanently visible (but harmless) zombie next to pods of villagers to keep that fear state active forever, then guides every spawned golem into a shaft where fall damage — not a glitch, an intended mechanic — kills it in one hit, dropping iron ingots and poppies straight into a hopper collection line.

Build outline

  1. Build several small villager "pods," each stocked with beds so the game recognizes a large enough village population.
  2. Place a zombie or zombie villager in a glass-walled cage where villagers can see it but it can never reach them.
  3. Leave open platforms above or beside the pods where golems have room to spawn.
  4. Line the spawning platform with water streams that gently guide new golems toward a central drop shaft.
  5. Make the shaft at least 23 blocks deep so the fall alone kills the golem on landing.
  6. Place a hopper line under the landing point to catch iron and poppy drops, feeding into a comparator-monitored storage chest.

Role of each component

PartRole in this machine
HopperCollects and transports every iron ingot and poppy to storage.
ComparatorReads the storage chest's fill level and can lock the hopper line before it overflows.
ObserverOptionally detects new drops entering the hopper line to trigger a status indicator.
Redstone LampLights up as an "active farm" indicator wired to the observer or comparator.
Redstone Torch / RepeaterBuild the small self-resetting clock that drives the optional status indicator.

The villager-fear spawning rule has been stable since Minecraft 1.14; this design has stayed reliable through 1.21 with only minor rate tweaks from version to version.

Fully Automated Raid Farm

Designerilmango
Best inJava 1.18 – 1.21
DifficultyExtreme
CategoryMob Farm

How it works

Killing an illager captain while the player has the Bad Omen effect starts a raid on the nearest village — an intended game mechanic, not an exploit. This farm baits every wave of that raid (pillagers, vindicators, witches, evokers, and ravagers) onto a minimal fake village stacked with spawning platforms, funnels the mobs down through water streams into a grinder, and automatically sorts the loot — emeralds, totems of undying, enchanted gear — into separate storage.

Build outline

  1. Establish the smallest structure the game recognizes as a "village" (a claimed bed and workstation) at the farm's base.
  2. Stack multiple open spawning platforms above the village, enclosed so raiders can only appear there.
  3. Line each platform with water streams angled toward a central vertical shaft.
  4. Build an observer- or comparator-driven clock that pulses pistons to open trapdoor "gates," releasing mobs toward the shaft in controlled batches instead of letting them jam up.
  5. Finish the shaft with a fall-damage drop into a one-hit kill point.
  6. Run a wide hopper floor under the kill point into comparator-sorted chests, separating emeralds and totems from common drops.
  7. The player triggers each raid manually by obtaining Bad Omen and killing a captain near the fake village — this step is player action, not redstone, but it's what keeps the farm running.

Role of each component

PartRole in this machine
ObserverDrives the timing clock that opens and closes each platform's mob-release gate.
PistonOperates the trapdoor gates, releasing raiders in a controlled rhythm so they don't stack up.
RepeaterFine-tunes the gate clock's pulse length so mobs have time to pass through each opening.
ComparatorReads chest fill levels to control sorting of emeralds, totems, and common drops into separate storage.
HopperThe loot-collection backbone running beneath the entire kill chamber.
Redstone TorchProvides the base inverted signal the self-resetting gate clock is built from.

Illager pathing and raid spawn rules have shifted slightly across versions since the Village & Pillage update (1.14); rebuilds are usually tuned for whichever current release the server is running.

Fully Automatic Melon & Pumpkin Farm

Designerilmango
Best inJava 1.17+
DifficultyEasy – Moderate
CategoryCrop Farm

How it works

Melon and pumpkin stems grow fruit into the empty block space beside them. An observer aimed at that space sees the block update the instant a fruit appears and fires a one-tick pulse, which triggers a piston to knock the fruit loose. Water underneath sweeps every harvested block into a single channel, where a looping hopper minecart gathers it all into storage — the entire farm runs with zero player interaction once switched on.

Build outline

  1. Plant rows of melon or pumpkin stems on farmland, leaving one open block beside each stem for the fruit to grow into.
  2. Face an observer at each of those empty blocks, output side away from the plant.
  3. Wire each observer's pulse to a normal piston positioned to push the grown fruit off its block the instant it appears.
  4. Cut a shallow water channel beneath the whole row so every knocked-loose fruit gets swept toward one collection point.
  5. Lay powered rail in a loop under the channel and run a hopper minecart continuously across it to gather the drifting items.
  6. Feed the minecart's haul into a chest monitored by a comparator, and wire a lever-controlled torch as a master shutoff for the whole observer network.

Role of each component

PartRole in this machine
ObserverDetects the instant a stem grows fruit and fires the harvest pulse.
PistonKnocks the newly grown melon or pumpkin off its block so it can be collected.
Hopper MinecartLoops beneath the channel, sweeping harvested fruit into storage.
ComparatorReads the storage chest's fill level to manage the collection cart.
RepeaterKeeps each observer's pulse timed correctly against its piston's extension.
Redstone Torch & LeverForm the master on/off switch that lets a player pause the whole farm.

The observer-piston harvest technique has worked since observers were added in 1.11; this specific tileable layout is documented and functioning on 1.17 and later.
